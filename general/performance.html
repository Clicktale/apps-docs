Performance

Performance

The Clicktale for Apps SDK is designed to run in the background and avoid causing any lags or delays to your application. Clicktale for Apps today is running on many large scale apps, generating billions of recordings per month transparently helping our enterprise customers deliver more revenue and better experiences.

Clicktale has been serving leading enterprises for over 10 years and is known as the pioneer in improving digital customer experiences. As such, we have put a lot of engineering effort into our SDK to ensure that when using Clicktale for Apps, the user experience will be improved – not hindered.

There are multiple mechanisms that control the behavior of the SDK in your application – all of them are fully configurable and controlled by the customer – you.

  • Via remote configuration, you can turn the SDK on or off at any time.
  • You can also remotely target the SDK to only record on specific devices, OS, or for specific versions of your app.

The benefit here is that you can change these settings without needing to release new versions of your application, as these are controlled by remote configuration. Moreover, this SDK was designed to be compatible with even very old versions of smartphones such as iPhone 4 and iPhone 5 hardware or Android 2.0 and 4.0. Note that support for older versions of Android and iOS may be deprecated in the future (in regards of OS version and SDK level)

While testing different hardware configurations, we find that on the oldest type of devices such as iPhone 4/5, performance demands will be up to 20%. This means that on these old types of devices you will see a slightly higher battery consumption when the SDK is running. Nonetheless – there will be no lag or latency inside the application, i.e. users will not feel that anything is impacting their experience.

On more modern iOS devices such as the iPhone 6/7 or other 2015 devices and more recent, we see very low performance demands and no noticeable impact by users.

On modern Android devices, the main load of generating the playback of the session is done after the app is closed and terminated - making it also completely transparent during actual usage.
